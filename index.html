Abia State, Nigeria

Abia State is located in the southeastern region of Nigeria. It's bordered by the states of Imo, Cross River, Rivers, Anambra, Enugu, and Ebonyi. Abia State was carved out of the former Imo State in 1991. The name "Abia" is an abbreviation of four of the state's densely populated regions Aba, Bende, Isuikwuato, and Afikpo. It is one of the thirty-six (36) States that constitute the Federal Republic of Nigeria. Abia people are of the Igbo ethnic group who predominates much of the Southeastern part of Nigeria. Their traditional language is Igbo. English is widely spoken and serves as the official language in governance and business. Geography & Location Abia State has tropical rainforests in the south and woodland savanna in the north. The Imo and Aba Rivers flow through the state. The state has sedimentary structures like mud cracks, cross-bedding, and burrows. Capital: The capital of Abia State is Umuahia. The most populous city in Abia State is Aba. Culture & Language: English is the official language of governance and business in Abia State Ibibio is a minority language spoken in Abia State, especially in Arochukwu Their culture includes art, music, and dance. Igbo culture is expressed in these forms of art Igbo culture is celebrated through traditional festivals They produce a traditional textile unique to the Akwete people in the Ukwa East local government area. Igbo language is the most widely spoken, but English is the official language. The people are known for their hospitality, traditional dances, masquerades, and colorful festivals. Economy & Natural Resources: Abia State is a major producer of oil palm, yams, cassava, and vegetables. The state is also rich in crude oil and gas. The state's chief industrial center is Aba, which manufactures textiles, pharmaceuticals, soap, plastics, cement, footwear, and cosmetics. Climate: The state has a tropical climate with heavy rainfall, especially from April to October. The state's yearly mean temperature is 78.1°F (25.6°C). Population: Abia State is predominantly inhabited by the Igbo people. The state is one of the most densely populated areas in Nigeria.
